# ahar0603_9301_capstone_idea_2

This project is an interactive p5.js installation prototype that explores the tension between visibility and obscurity. Using a live camera feed, the work transforms the viewer's image into a softened, rain-covered, frosted-glass surface. The screen behaves like a temporary membrane: when the viewer clicks and drags, they gently wipe away the condensation and reveal fragments of the image beneath.

The interaction is intentionally subtle and impermanent. Each cleared mark slowly fades back into the fog, creating a continuous cycle of reveal and conceal. This slow disappearance encourages the viewer to keep interacting, while also making the image feel unstable, fragile, and atmospheric.

## Installation Concept

The work is designed to be experienced as a screen-based interactive installation. A webcam captures the viewer in real time, while the display presents their image as if seen through misted glass or a rain-covered window. The viewer interacts directly with the surface by using a mouse, trackpad, or touch input to wipe parts of the fog away.

In a physical installation setting, the piece could be presented on a large monitor, projection, or touch screen. The camera would be positioned near the display so the viewer encounters a delayed and obscured reflection of themselves. The act of wiping becomes a gesture of searching, revealing, and losing visual clarity again as the surface gradually refogs.
